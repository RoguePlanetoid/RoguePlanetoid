RoguePlanetoid Weekly Update #83

21st July 2025

This month I found out I had been renewed as a Microsoft MVP, and it was amazing to find out I was able to remain a Microsoft Most Valuable Professional. Everything I have done including Presentations, Workshops, Packages and more for the community has helped me achieve this yet again and am grateful for the support I have had and continue to get from others in the community, there's plenty to look forward to such as .NET 10 and so much more for developers in the .NET and Windows ecosystem.

This month I've been working on updates to many of my Packages and over the next couple of weeks I'll be publishing those updates to Packages which includes Toolkits and Assets starting this week. It has been great to work on the new functionality, updates and more and am looking forward to seeing how they may be used, so if you're using my Packages then let me know as I'd love to feature them here!

With these updates to my Packages completed I'll be working on putting together something I've been wanting to do for a while to help people get their kickstart in programming. I've been able to help many people over the years with tutorials and workshops as well as presentations, but this will be more in-depth and hands on and can't wait to talk more about this very soon but suffice to say there will be new ways to learn from me.

Renewed Microsoft MVP for .NET & Windows Development

10th July 2025

Today it was fantastic to see that I had been renewed as a Most Valuable Professional from Microsoft for .NET and Windows Development. The MVP Award recognises exceptional contributions to the Microsoft technical community by sharing expertise including performing presentations, running workshops, writing articles and recording podcasts along with a commitment to solving issues that impact people in their lives. I remain honoured and humbled to have been renewed as a Microsoft MVP and continue to share articles and podcast episodes here or tutorials, talks and workshops at tutorialr.com along with packages for developers and more at comentsys.com.
