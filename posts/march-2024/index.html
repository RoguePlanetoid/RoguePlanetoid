RoguePlanetoid Weekly Update #50

31st March 2024

It is amazing to reach weekly update number fifty and this week started with a fun post on X, formerly known as Twitter, where I saw the following post:

oh my god this is literally the PSD they used for the cover pic.twitter.com/L9ZH6HKzww

— Sura (@Surasia_) March 23, 2024

It showed a video of them showing the PhotoShop file that had been used for the cover of Halo 3 and it was great to see this image broken down like that, so realised I had the PhotoShop file for the cover of the original Halo Combat Evolved I decided to share a similar video in the following post:

Inspired by @Surasia_ sharing the cover PSD for Halo 3 on Xbox, here's my video of the PSD for the cover for the OG Halo Combat Evolved game for Xbox! pic.twitter.com/3RVDNurmXW

— Peter Bull (@RoguePlanetoid) March 24, 2024

That post ended up being the most engaged post I've ever done there with 450 likes, 68 reposts, 124 bookmarks and over 27,000 views! It was great to be able to show this image off to the community just like Surasia_ did and great to share something else that the community hadn't seen before and now have that Halo 3 image in my collection too! I couldn't believe the reaction that post have and may never see that again but is closest to going viral I've ever been but was great to share in that moment with the Xbox, Halo, and Microsoft community!

This week I also attended the first anniversary celebration of Silicon Mingle here in the North East at the Town Wall in Newcastle upon Tyne on Thursday, it was amazing meeting new faces and catching up with familiar ones. I've really enjoyed going along to the event and help celebrate with some cake and even a party hat! Silicon Mingle attracts a wide range of people at various stages of their business from planning, just starting out, growing and even those who have exited and working on the next big thing for them. Silicon Mingle has been an amazing event to learn what others are doing in the North East tech scene and really pleased to play my small part in that space and always look forward to going along and hope to see many more years of this amazing event to come! It is also great to hear that it has expanded to Manchester, where I attended an event last year and was amazed at the change and progress that had happened in the decade since I had last been there and hope that Newcastle upon Tyne, Gateshead and other places in the North East are well on their way to becoming like that with new developments happening all the time it does feel like we're on that path and events like Silicon Mingle will help make that happen!

Next week will be the fifteenth episode of the RoguePlanetoid Podcast about .NET MAUI, something I used for my recent talk, Take a Note of XAML with .NET 8, for the first time when putting together the demo for it, it is interesting using it after having some experience using Xamarin a few years ago but there was many features and functionality missing that I'm pleased to say are now in the platform. I'm looking forward to using .NET MAUI for more things in the future and helps continue my series of Episodes about XAML which will conclude with an episode on Uno Platform in May!

tutorialr.com Tutorial Tuesday - Windows App SDK - Set Eighteen

12th March 2024

It is the last Tutorial Tuesday for now and tutorialr.com has been updated with four new Tutorials for Windows App SDK that you can follow to create some custom looking controls.

Custom RadioButton
Custom RatingControl
Custom TextBox
Custom ComboBox

Customised Controls include Custom RadioButton which applied a custom look to a RadioButton, Custom RatingControl which customises the look of a RatingControl, Custom TextBox which changes the appearance of a TextBox and Custom ComboBox for a custom looking ComboBox.

There will be more Tutorials for Windows App SDK coming later this year, which will include mini-apps to create your own small applications along with more complex examples to help understand and implement a variety of functionality in Windows App SDK which will eventually complete the set of one-hundred Tutorials!
